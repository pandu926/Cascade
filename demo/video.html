The problem

AI agents compose skills to get things done.
But the skill economy has no incentive layer.

Skills are built on top of other skills — translation, summarization, reasoning, retrieval. The deeper the composition, the more powerful the agent.
But today: there's no mechanism to reward the entire stack. Only the top skill gets paid. The foundations earn nothing.

Why this breaks everything

Without economic incentives, nobody builds reusable skills.

Developers build monoliths instead — big, non-composable, all revenue to themselves.
No trust mechanism for revenue sharing. No automation. No transparency.
The result: an AI ecosystem that can't scale through composition.

The solution

Cascade: the economic layer for composable skills.

One invoke — every contributor in the dependency chain gets paid automatically.
Recursive. Trustless. Immutable. One transaction. Zero intermediaries.

What this enables:

Every skill you build becomes a permanent revenue source — as long as someone, somewhere in the ecosystem, uses something built on top of yours. Code is the contract. No platform fees. No middleman.

Step 1 · Register

Alice creates a translation skill.

She registers it on-chain. It's a leaf — no dependencies. Immutable once registered. No one can change her terms later.

Why this matters:

Alice's skill becomes a permanent, composable building block. Anyone can build on top of it — and she'll earn from every use downstream.

$ cast send CASCADE "register(0, [], [])" --rpc-url mainnet

→ tx: 0x36d545ef...a74f4d

→ status: success ✓

→ Alice's skill = id 7 (leaf, no deps)

Step 2 · Compose

Bob builds a summarizer using Alice's skill.

He declares: "40% of my revenue goes to Alice." This is enforced by the contract — not by trust, not by a platform.

Why this matters:

Bob acknowledges Alice's contribution at registration time. It's permanent. If someone uses Bob's skill, Alice automatically earns her share.

$ cast send CASCADE "register(0, [7], [4000])"

# depends on Alice (id 7), gives her 40%

→ tx: 0xabf1928b...764c00

→ status: success ✓

→ Bob's skill = id 8 (depends on Alice)

Step 3 · Compose deeper

Carol builds an AI agent using Bob's summarizer.

She sets a price: 0.005 PROS per use. And declares 50% goes to Bob's subtree (which includes Alice).

The composition grows:

Carol → Bob → Alice. Three layers deep. When Carol's agent gets paid, the money automatically flows up — no extra code needed.

$ cast send CASCADE "register(5000000000000000, [8], [5000])"

# price: 0.005 PROS, depends Bob (id 8), gives 50%

→ tx: 0xf8b6deba...9af9c2f

→ status: success ✓

→ Carol's skill = id 9 (depends on Bob → Alice)

Step 4 · One invoke

Someone pays to use Carol's agent. 0.005 PROS.

One transaction. The payment cascades recursively through the entire dependency tree:

Carol keeps her share. Bob gets his. Alice — at the very bottom — gets hers too. Automatically.

$ cast send CASCADE "invoke(9)" --value 0.005ether

→ tx: 0xe34d0e39...f128a8

→ status: success ✓

# Check who got paid:

Carol (top): +0.0025 PROS (50% kept)

Bob (mid): +0.0015 PROS (60% of 50%)

Alice (leaf): +0.0010 PROS (40% of Bob's share)

Σ = 0.005 PROS ✓ (conservation exact)

Step 5 · Claim

Alice withdraws her royalties. Anytime she wants.

No approval needed. No platform fee. No middleman.
Pull-payment: her money, her schedule.

Security by design:

Pull-payment means no one can block Alice's earnings. Even if Carol disappears, Alice's share is already accrued in the contract — waiting for her.

$ cast call CASCADE "balances(alice)"

→ 1000000000000000 wei (0.001 PROS accrued)
